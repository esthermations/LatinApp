==Latin==

===Etymology 1===
From Old Latin ''hoc'', ''hocc'', from *''hod-ce'', from {{inh|la|itc-pro|*hod}}, neuter nominative/accusative singular of {{m|itc-pro|*ho||this}}, from [[Proto-Indo-European]] {{m|ine-pro|*gʰe}} + Proto-Indo-European {{m|ine-pro|*ḱe||here}}.

====Pronunciation====
* {{la-IPA|hoc}}
* Note: before a vowel, the final [k] is always doubled in poetry, e.g. using Classical pronunciation, ''hoc est'' [hɔkk ɛst]. W. Sydney Allen also reports an attested ancient ''occest''. Contrast {{l|la|hic}}, which can be [hɪk ɛst] or [hɪkk ɛst] in this position.

===Etymology 3===
According to De Vaan (2008), from a masculine singular instrumental of {{inh|la|ine-pro||*gʰi-ḱe|this, here}}. Contrast {{noncog|la|hūc}}, which is probably from the locative instead.
