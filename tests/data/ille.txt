===Etymology===
From {{inh|la|itc-ola|olle|t=he, that}} (also ''ollus'', ''olla''), from {{der|la|ine-pro|*h₂ol-no-}} or {{m|ine-pro|*h₂l̥-no-}}, from {{m|ine-pro|*h₂el-|t=beyond, other}}. Cognate with Latin {{doublet|la|uls|t1=beyond|alius|t2=other|alter|t3=the other|notext=1}}; {{cog|xum|ulu|t=to that place}}, {{cog|cu|лани|t=last year|lit=in that (year)}}.

Initial ''i-'' from ''o-'' has no parallel case and may be owing to contamination from {{m|la|is}}, {{m|la|iste}} or due to the palatalizing effect of ''l exilis''.

===Usage notes===
* This demonstrative determiner/pronoun is used to refer to a person or thing, or persons or things, away from both speaker and listener. It contrasts with {{m|la|hic||this}}, which refers to people or things near the speaker, and {{m|la|iste||this/that}}, which refers to people or things near the listener.
* As Latin had no person pronouns specifically meaning "he", "she" or "it", any of {{m|la|ille}}, {{m|la|iste}}, {{m|la|hic}} or (most frequently) {{m|la|is}} could assume that function. In Vulgar latin, {{m|la|ille}} weakened its meaning and frequently came to mean merely "the" (as a determiner) or "he/she/it" (as a pronoun). This is in fact the origin of {{cog|fr|le||the}} and {{m|fr|il||he}}, {{cog|es|el||the}} and {{m|es|él||he}}, etc. The original meaning of a far demonstrative was maintained when augmented with {{m|la|ecce}} or {{m|la|eccum}}, cf. {{cog|it|quello}}, {{cog|es|aquel}}.
* In Classical usage, {{m|la|ille}} can have a secondary, appreciative function of casting the referent in a positive light: {{m|la|ille homō}} can mean "that (famous/renowned) man". The opposite, pejorative function is assumed by {{m|la|iste}}, and {{m|la|iste homō}} frequently means "that (no good) man". Such functions were not present in Vulgar Latin, and {{m|la|iste}} came to mean "this" (cf. {{cog|es|este}}, {{cog|pt|este}}).
