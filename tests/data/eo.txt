=====Conjugation=====
Irregular, but similar to [[Appendix:Latin fourth conjugation|fourth conjugation]]. The third principal part {{m|la|iī}} occasionally appears as {{m|la|īvī}} in Plautus, but never in Cicero, Caesar, Sallust, or Livy. The perfect active infinitive and pluperfect subjunctive stem {{m|la|īsse}} occurs twice as {{m|la|iisse}} in the PHI corpus.<ref>Weiss, Michael. (2009) Outline of the Historical and Comparative Grammar of Latin, p. 429</ref>

===Etymology 2===
Declined from {{m|la|is}}. Somewhere it stands as if for ''eō locō/tempore'' ("this/that place/time, there, till"), somewhere it stand as if for ''eō modō'' ("this/that mode/way, thus"). Compare {{m|la|eā}}.
